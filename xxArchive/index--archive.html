Checkers 2: The Sequel to Checkers: It's About Girls This Time
2019, tabletop roleplaying game
solo project

Checkers 2 is a very small romance tabletop roleplaying game which takes a slightly modified game of checkers as a metaphor for two lesbians flirting. The game is as much about strategy as it is about bluffing-- watch your opponent carefully, you might learn more than you expect from a slight hesitation or pursed lips.

Signs & Wonders
2020, tabletop roleplaying game
Designed in collaboration with Robin Gibson
my roles: design, writing, layout, illustration, facilitation

Signs & Wonders is a tabletop roleplaying game where players utilize a tarot deck to imagine the events leading up to a cataclysm of their invention. The game begins with deciding what the inevitable catastrophe is, & then players spend the next 1-2 hours interpreting the signals & portents which lead up to the end of everything.

The game was made during the lead up to the first outbreaks of COVID-19 in the United States, & is also an earnest expression of grief in the slow crawl of climate change.

no smoke
2021, live action roleplaying game
Designed in collaboration with Beau McGhee
my roles: design, writing, layout, illustration

no smoke is a very, very short live action roleplaying game about Icarus, the sun, & (not) kissing. Originally made as 200 word TTRPG, the game found a new home in a trifold zine when we were invited to exhibit it at the Wonderville venue in Brooklyn, New York. Beau & I split the design, each covering roughly half of the content, & I was additionally responsible for layout & illustration.

Under Advisement
2021, videocall roleplaying
Designed in collaboration with Dylan Cruz, Katie McLain, & Jen Bourke
my roles: design, writing, layout, facilitation

During the pandemic, a few friends & I got together to make a game which was supposed to be played over Zoom. The resulting game was Under Advisement, which is a light roleplaying game for 4-6 players. Players take on one of several roles-- the King, the Knight, or the Advisors. The King is trying to make policy decisions for their country, the Advisors are all trying to push their agendas through Zoom's chat feature (which the King can't see), & the Knight has to be the bridge between them.

dumpling.love
2021-2022 (on going), browser game
Designed in collaboration with Mut Moochi, Fran Rojo, Darwin Vickers, Ty Cobb, Jen Bourke, & The Parks Staff
my roles: design, narrative/writing, UI, visual design, research, facilitation

dumpling.love is a browser-based adventure game which revels in the material of the internet. The game itself is a massive, navigatable web-collage built by a team of 7. As players click from page to page of the website, they push deeper into an absurd park & meet its many denizens.

When our team first began developing the game, we were interested in what makes a website a website. We were also interested in experimenting with developing a game within a flat hierarchy; there we no project managers or defined roles, we each volunteered what we were able to. The result is an award-winning adventure game which still receives updates to this day.

last november
2019, interactive poem collection
solo project

last november is an interactive poetry collection which allows readers to toggle any word in any of the poems on or off. The goal here is to allow a reader to build their own poems out of the poems provided through the "erasure" method of poetry composition.
